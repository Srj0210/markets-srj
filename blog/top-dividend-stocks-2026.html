Top 10 Dividend Stocks for Passive Income in 2026

Updated April 9, 2026 · 9 min read

Looking to build a passive income stream from dividends? Here are 10 high-quality dividend stocks with reliable payouts and strong fundamentals.

1. Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) — Healthcare

62+ years of consecutive dividend increases. One of only two companies with AAA credit rating. Diversified across pharma, medical devices, and consumer health. View JNJ →

2. Coca-Cola (KO) — Consumer Staples

62+ years of dividend increases. Warren Buffett's favorite stock. Strong global brand with pricing power. View KO →

3. Procter & Gamble (PG) — Consumer Staples

68+ years of dividend increases. Owns Tide, Gillette, Pampers, and dozens of household brands. View PG →

4. Realty Income (O) — REIT

Monthly dividend payer (not quarterly!). 100+ consecutive quarterly dividend increases. Known as "The Monthly Dividend Company." View O →

5. PepsiCo (PEP) — Consumer Staples

51+ years of dividend increases. More diversified than Coca-Cola with snack brands (Frito-Lay, Doritos). View PEP →

6. Microsoft (MSFT) — Technology

Rapidly growing dividend backed by massive free cash flow from cloud (Azure) and Office 365. View MSFT →

7. Verizon (VZ) — Telecom

High yield (~6-7%) with stable cash flows from wireless subscribers. View VZ →

8. McDonald's (MCD) — Consumer Discretionary

48+ years of dividend increases. Asset-light franchise model generates reliable cash flow. View MCD →

9. AbbVie (ABBV) — Healthcare

High yield (~4-5%) with strong pharma pipeline. Humira replacement drugs showing solid growth. View ABBV →

10. Apple (AAPL) — Technology

Lower yield (~0.5%) but massive buybacks and growing dividend. $100B+ annual free cash flow. View AAPL →
