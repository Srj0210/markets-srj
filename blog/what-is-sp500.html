What is the S&P 500? Complete Guide for Beginners

Updated April 9, 2026 · 7 min read

The S&P 500 is arguably the most important stock market index in the world. If you're new to investing, understanding the S&P 500 is essential. Here's everything you need to know.

What Does S&P 500 Mean?

S&P stands for Standard & Poor's, the company that created the index. The "500" refers to the approximately 500 largest publicly traded companies in the United States, weighted by market capitalization.

Top 10 Holdings (as of 2026)

The S&P 500 is weighted by market cap, so the largest companies have the biggest impact:

Apple (AAPL), Microsoft (MSFT), NVIDIA (NVDA), Amazon (AMZN), Alphabet (GOOGL), Meta (META), Tesla (TSLA), Berkshire Hathaway, Eli Lilly, Broadcom.

Historical Returns

Since 1926, the S&P 500 has returned an average of about 10% per year (before inflation). In real terms (after inflation), the return is about 7% per year. This makes it one of the best long-term wealth-building tools available.

How to Invest in the S&P 500

You can't buy the index directly, but you can buy ETFs that track it: SPY, VOO, or IVV. Read our detailed comparison of S&P 500 ETFs.
