How to Read Stock Charts: Beginner's Complete Guide

Updated April 9, 2026 · 12 min read

Understanding stock charts is one of the most valuable skills for any investor or trader. This guide covers everything you need to know about reading charts, from basic candlestick patterns to advanced indicators.

1. Types of Charts

Line Charts: Connect closing prices with a line. Simple and clean — great for seeing overall trends.

Candlestick Charts: Show open, high, low, and close for each period. Green/white candles = price went up. Red/black candles = price went down. The "body" shows open-to-close range, "wicks" show high and low.

You can switch between line and candlestick views on any stock page on SRJahir Markets.

2. Support and Resistance

Support: A price level where buying pressure tends to prevent further decline. Think of it as a "floor."

Resistance: A price level where selling pressure prevents further advance. Think of it as a "ceiling."

When price breaks through resistance, it often becomes new support (and vice versa). This is called a "breakout."

3. Moving Averages

Moving averages smooth out price data to show trends. The most common are the 50-day and 200-day moving averages. When the 50-day crosses above the 200-day, it's called a "Golden Cross" (bullish). When it crosses below, it's a "Death Cross" (bearish).

4. Volume

Volume shows how many shares were traded. High volume on an up day confirms buyer conviction. High volume on a down day confirms seller conviction. Low volume moves are less reliable.

5. RSI (Relative Strength Index)

RSI ranges from 0-100. Above 70 = overbought (may pull back). Below 30 = oversold (may bounce). RSI is useful for timing entries and exits.
