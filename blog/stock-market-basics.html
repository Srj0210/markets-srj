Stock Market Basics: How the US Market Works

Updated April 9, 2026 · 8 min read

New to the stock market? This guide explains everything you need to know about how the US stock market works, from buying your first stock to understanding market mechanics.

What is the Stock Market?

The stock market is where buyers and sellers trade shares of publicly listed companies. When you buy a stock, you're buying a small piece of ownership in that company.

NYSE vs NASDAQ

The two major US stock exchanges are the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and NASDAQ. NYSE is the world's largest exchange by market cap. NASDAQ is known for tech companies. Both operate from 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM Eastern Time.

Types of Orders

Market Order: Buy/sell immediately at the current price. Fastest but you don't control the exact price.

Limit Order: Buy/sell only at your specified price or better. You control the price but it may not execute.

Stop-Loss Order: Automatically sell if price drops to a specified level. Protects you from large losses.

Key Terms

Market Cap = Share Price × Total Shares. P/E Ratio = Price / Earnings Per Share. Dividend Yield = Annual Dividend / Share Price. Volume = Number of shares traded.

How to Start Investing

1. Open a brokerage account (Fidelity, Schwab, Interactive Brokers, etc.)
2. Fund your account
3. Research stocks or start with an S&P 500 ETF like VOO
4. Place your first order
5. Think long-term — don't panic sell
