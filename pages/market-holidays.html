How Market Holidays Affect Trading

When US markets are closed, you cannot buy or sell stocks on NYSE or NASDAQ. However, futures markets may still be open with limited hours. Options expiring on a holiday are typically settled the business day before.

Trading volume tends to be lower on days before and after holidays, which can lead to increased volatility. Many professional traders reduce their positions before long holiday weekends.

International Market Holidays

If you trade stocks on international exchanges, note that different countries have different holiday schedules. The London Stock Exchange, Tokyo Stock Exchange, and other markets each follow their own national holiday calendar.
