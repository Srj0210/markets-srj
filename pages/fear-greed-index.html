What is the Fear and Greed Index?

The Fear and Greed Index measures market sentiment on a scale of 0 (Extreme Fear) to 100 (Extreme Greed). It combines several indicators including market volatility (VIX), trading volume, put/call ratios, and market momentum.

How to Use the Fear & Greed Index

"Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful." — Warren Buffett

When the index shows Extreme Fear (0-25), it often means stocks are oversold and may present buying opportunities. When it shows Extreme Greed (75-100), the market may be overvalued and due for a pullback.

However, this is just one tool — never make investment decisions based solely on sentiment indicators. Check our stock chart analysis guide for more tools.
