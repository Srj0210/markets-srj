SIP/DCA Investment Calculator

See how much your regular monthly investments will grow over time with compound returns. Perfect for planning your Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA) strategy in the US stock market.

How Does DCA Work in the Stock Market?

Dollar Cost Averaging means investing a fixed dollar amount at regular intervals — say $500 every month into an S&P 500 ETF like VOO or SPY. When prices are low, your $500 buys more shares. When prices are high, you buy fewer shares. Over time, this averages out your cost basis and removes the stress of timing the market.

Real Example: $500/month in S&P 500 for 20 Years

At the historical average return of ~10% per year, investing $500 per month for 20 years would turn $120,000 of total contributions into approximately $382,000 — that's $262,000 in pure investment gains from compound returns.
